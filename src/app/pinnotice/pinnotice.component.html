PURPOSE OF THIS PATIENT INFORMATION NOTICE

This Patient Information Notice (PIN) is intended to ask you for your consent to allow healthcare professionals in Ireland to access your Patient Summary through MyHealth@EU

MyHealth@EU enables safe and easy access to your health data for healthcare professionals involved in your treatment and the provision of medicines - anytime and anywhere within the EU /EEA. This is done by electronic means through secure gateways provided by National Contact Points for eHealth (NCPeH) designated by each country. Pages 2 and 3 of this notice inform you about the processing of your personal data and your rights. This PIN aims to inform patients regarding the exchange of their data in cross-border settings and the data protection practices that are in place.

When seeking treatment in a healthcare provider organisation in Ireland, patient personal data will be handled in accordance with the infrastructure and laws of Ireland. Please note that consent is only used as a lawful basis for limited purposes i.e. Use of the MyHealth@EU service where patient summary data is shared with healthcare professionals with your consent. For more information on the lawful basis relied upon by the HSE for processing personal information please visit hse-privacy notice-service-users.pdfopen_in_new

This information is intended for patients whose country of residence is within the EU / EAA.

CONSENT FORM

By signing this form, you are confirming that you consent to the processing of your personal data for the following purposes:

  • For the healthcare professional to consult the National Contact Point Ireland (NCP) in order to access your health data in your country of residence
  • For the NCP to process your personal information to confirm that your Patient Summary is available through the eHealth Digital Services Infrastructure
    • liFor the NCP to deliver your available health data to the consulting healthcare professional in Ireland

WHO CAN USE THIS SERVICE?

MyHealth@EU enables safe and easy access to medical data for healthcare professionals involved in treatment.

This infrastructure provides healthcare professionals access to medical data for residents of the EU / EEA by electronic means through a secure gateway provided by a National Contact Point for eHealth (NCPeH) designated by each country.

The HSE is the designated NCPeH in Ireland and assumes responsibility as a data controller for the collection, storage, transfer and other processing activities of the data in its medical registry.

This also means that your personal data is recorded, transferred and stored in accordance with the laws of Ireland.

WHAT DATA ABOUT YOU IS PROCESSED?

A Patient Summary contains the most important information about your health is collected from the country where it is stored, such as your home country, in order to use it for your treatment in another country. The patient summary includes information on important patient data such as allergies, current medication, previous illnesses and surgeries that are necessary to treat you properly abroad.

WHAT IS THE LEGAL BASIS FOR THE USE OF YOUR PERSONAL DATA?

Your personal data is transferred, processed and stored in accordance with the provisions of the GDPR, Directive 2011/24/EU on the application of patientsâ€™ rights in cross-border healthcare, the agreement between the EU Member States and the Agreement between National Authorities or National Organisations responsible for National Contact Points for eHealth The HSE relies on Article 9(2)(h) to process special category data in its provision of healthcare services, i.e. for the purposes of preventative or occupational medicine, for the assessment of the working capacity of an employee, for medical diagnosis, for the provision of medical care, treatment or social care, for the management of health or social care systems and services. Finally, in accordance with Articles 6, 7 and 9 of the GDPR, MyHealth@U s will be available only with your explicit prior consent. If you did not give your explicit consent before travelling and/or before care is actually to be provided to you in Ireland, your data will not be available via MyHealth@EU to the healthcare provider, not even in an emergency.

FOR WHAT PURPOSE IS YOUR DATA PROCESSED?

Your health data will only be used for the provision of your health care. No secondary use or processing of your patient summary data is intended.

WHO PROCESSES AND HAS ACCESS TO THIS DATA?

Your Patient Summary data will be accessible only by authorized and identifiable health professionals in Ireland involved in your treatment and are bound by the obligation of professional secrecy. The HSE as the National Contact Point for eHealth, has access only to the administrative data related to your identity, which must be verified by the health professional in Ireland as part of the request for the Patient Summary.

Each country of treatment participating in MyHealth@EU has undertaken to ensure that the participating health professionals and healthcare providers on their territory have adequate information and training about their duties, and in particular to fulfil the patient information obligation under Article 13 GDPR

The Patient Summary data will be transferred using a secure technical gateway provided by MyHealth@EU.

WHERE AND HOW LONG IS THE PERSONAL DATA STORED?

As a general rule data must be retained for no longer than is necessary for the purpose for which it was collected in the first place. The NCP only stores the consent forms which will be stored for 5 years. Both the healthcare provider and the NCP can delete all, or parts, of your personal data upon your request. See point 7 for more details about your privacy rights and how to invoke them.

YOUR ACCESS RIGHTS

If you consent for your Patient Summary data to be processed by MyHealth@EU, you can obtain a copy of it by sending a request to The HSE or the healthcare provider in the country of treatment.

You have the right to:

  1. Rectify any inaccurate or incomplete data in your Patient Summary, according to GDPR Article 16
  2. Data Requests - HSE.ie
  3. Obtain the erasure of your Patient Summary data without undue delay, according to GDPR Article 17 GDPR Information - HSE.ie
  4. Object to the processing of your Patient Summary data on grounds relating to your particular situation, according to GDPR Article 21 Data Requests - HSE.ie
  5. Withdraw your consent at any time by contacting Data Requests - HSE.ie

Note that the Irish data controller may not have access to all your data for example those held in private practices and as such you would need to contact them directly regarding your data.

If you do not consent for your personal data to be processed by the eHDSI system, your data will not be available for you through this system when you are in another country, not even in case of an emergency.

Finally, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in Ireland or in your home country of treatment. Contact details of the supervisory authorities in each Member State can be found at the European Data Protection Boardopen_in_new
